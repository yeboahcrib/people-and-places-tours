Kente Weaving Village

Full Day Departure: Kumasi, Ghana From: $115/person
Tour Overview

Where Every Thread Tells a Story

Bonwire is the spiritual home of Kente cloth — the royal fabric of the Ashanti people that has become one of Ghana's most recognized symbols worldwide. This full-day journey takes you deep into the Ashanti region to visit master weavers in their family compounds, watch the mesmerizing rhythm of the traditional hand loom, and understand the encoded language of color and pattern that each piece carries.

You'll have the chance to try your hand at the loom (harder than it looks), commission a custom-woven piece to take home, and browse the village market for authentic Kente at fair prices. The day pairs the weaving village with a visit to the Kumasi Cultural Center, giving you the full context of Ashanti artisanship.

Fun Facts

  1. Kente cloth originated among the Ashanti of Ghana in the 17th century. Legend says the first weavers learned the art by observing a spider weave its web.
  2. Every Kente pattern has a name and meaning — colors and geometric designs encode proverbs, historical events, and social status. Gold represents royalty and wealth; blue represents love and harmony.
  3. Bonwire, the Kente weaving capital, is just 25 km from Kumasi. Entire families here have been weavers for generations, with children learning the craft from as young as age 7.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

Where is the Kente village? ▼
Bonwire — the spiritual home of Kente cloth — sits about 25km from Kumasi in the Ashanti region, roughly 4 hours from Accra.
Can I try weaving myself? ▼
Yes. You'll sit at a traditional hand loom under a master weaver's guidance. It's harder than it looks and one of those experiences you'll talk about for years.
Can I buy Kente directly from the weavers? ▼
Yes, and this is the best place to do it. Prices are fair and the pieces are authentic — not the tourist-market reproductions you find in Accra.
Can I commission a custom piece? ▼
Yes. Specify your pattern, colors, and size and the weavers will make it for you. Allow a few days for completion; we can arrange shipping if needed.
What else do we see? ▼
The tour pairs Bonwire with the Kumasi Cultural Center, giving you full context for Ashanti artisanship and its place in Ghanaian culture.
What's the group size? ▼
Small groups of 2–10 people. Intimate enough for a real experience.
